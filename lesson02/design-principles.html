Design Principles

Rule of Thirds

Nike

  • Nike.com

    • Nike is a great example of using the rule of thirds to ensure that they are grabbing the viewers attention to their main focal point. They have AJ Brown holding the football decked out in Nike gear as the main focal point. They have a nice balance of white space and their main image. Their website is aesthetically pleasing.

    Hick's Law

    Amazon

  • Amazon.com

    • Hick's law is present in Amazon's website by showing the users mulitiple choices of items that they are searching for. They have several features that help consumers have a great shopping experience. Some of those are:

    1- The navigation bar: to help shoppers find their items easier within categories.

    2- The seach bar: the search bar limits the number of options viewed by the consumer to help them to make a decision on what to purchase.

    3- Checkout: the checkout process can be a 1-click order or it can be a multi-step process that is easy and frustration free.

    White Space and Clean Design

    Apple

  • Apple.com

    • Apple is a good example of using white space to intentionally draw the users eyes to the specific elements of design. This is placed strategically to highlight their products. Their website is clean, elegant, and easy to navigate.
